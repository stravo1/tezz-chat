# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

tezz.chat is for power AI users: builders, researchers, developers, students, and operators who move quickly between models, chats, files, search, and generated outputs. They use the product in high-context work sessions where speed, control, persistence, and trust matter more than onboarding theater.

Primary users bring their own mental model of AI tools. They want fast conversation, clear model choice, local-first history, branching, attachments, web search, shareable threads, and the option to bring their own provider keys without feeling locked into one vendor.

## Product Purpose

tezz.chat is an open-source, multi-model AI chat app for advanced conversation workflows. It exists to make model switching, chat history, branching, file-aware prompts, image generation, web search, and shareable conversations feel immediate and dependable.

Success means a user can pick the right model, ask with the right context, inspect or fork a prior answer, continue across devices, and share the result without the interface becoming the work. The brand should communicate a fast, capable, AI-native tool for people who already know what they want to do.

## Brand Personality

Technical, dense, precise.

The product should feel closer to T3 Chat and Cursor than to a generic assistant landing page: sharp, compact, capable, direct, and made for people in motion. The voice is factual and pragmatic, with enough confidence to feel distinctive but no hype, mascots, or cute chatbot affect.

## Anti-references

Do not make tezz.chat feel like a generic SaaS landing page with vague gradients, interchangeable feature cards, hero metrics, or buzzword polish.

Do not make it feel like a toy chatbot with childish mascot energy, gimmicky bubbles, or unserious AI decoration.

Do not make it feel like an enterprise dashboard with sterile blue-gray sameness, heavy admin scaffolding, or corporate compliance aesthetics.

Do not make it feel like an overbuilt devtool through terminal cosplay, excessive monospace, or complexity that exists only to signal technical taste.

## Design Principles

Speed is the brand. Every surface should reduce the time between intent, model choice, answer, and next action.

Dense without being cryptic. Power features should be close at hand, but grouped and labeled so users can build muscle memory instead of decoding the UI.

Control should feel native. Model selection, BYOK setup, branching, sharing, attachments, search, and image mode should read as first-class capabilities, not bolted-on extras.

Trust comes from legibility. State, loading, errors, permissions, sync, privacy, and public sharing must be explicit enough that users understand what is happening before they act.

Open-source confidence over startup gloss. The product can be polished and distinctive, but it should stay direct, inspectable, and useful rather than performative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AAA where practical, with WCAG AA as the non-negotiable baseline for production surfaces.

Prioritize strong text contrast, keyboard-reachable workflows, visible focus states, readable density at chat scale, semantic controls, reduced-motion alternatives, and clear state labels for streaming, loading, errors, disabled provider models, attachments, and public/private chat visibility.
